Self Reflection AI Project

During this project I used Codex AI. I gotta be honest, writing the prompts is hard, and the AI doesn’t always listen even when making things as clear as possible. But that’s why you gotta improvise. The biggest problem I’ve had so far is that the AI is bad at putting things in the right location. For example, the AI I used was able to create things itself, but it was always misplaced. So, I eventually ended up making a map myself and even then it struggled. Because for example with doors, there would be a gap after opening them. But for the most part the AI listened very well. But that’s also because I wrote some detailed prompts. I had a big idea in mind so writing the prompts took pretty long. And I wasn’t able to fully finish the game because of that. But it already plays very well. Things function and work. It sometimes looks a little strange though. But AI is mostly for code, not designing, so it’s understandable.

What I think could have gone better for me is spending less time trying to perfectionize 1 thing and just move on to the next. I can always improve later, I have to get the rest ready first. But instead I wasted my time in trying to make everything perfect from the start. I think I also wasted a bit too much time thinking about what I wanted to write next. I had the idea in mind, but, I have to think a lot before I actually start writing since I try my best to say everything in as much detail as I can. I should try to make things less complicated for myself and trust a little bit more in the AI’s capabilities.

Things I could improve on during the lessons. I do think I’m doing things pretty well over all but I can definitely improve on some other parts besides how I do my work. During class I tend to easily get distracted by some of my classmates. And sometimes I can’t fully concentrate on what’s happening and being talked about during class because I’m spaced out or doing something else. Whenever there’s an explanation I should take my headphones off and listen. And during the lesson I gotta stop getting distracted.
